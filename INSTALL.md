# Installing Lumina

*The honest version: you'll click through two scary-looking warnings, one
boring installer, and one friendly setup wizard. Most of the time is
downloads. Total: 20–40 minutes, almost all of it waiting, none of it hard.*

---

## Before you start

- **Windows 11 or 10** (64-bit)
- **8 GB of memory** or more
- **About 15 GB of free disk** (her, a brain, and a voice)
- **Internet, today only.** Setup day is the one day she needs it — she has
  to fetch her brain and voice. After that, offline is fine forever.

## Step 1 — Download her

Your Gumroad receipt (also in your Gumroad Library) has the download:
**Lumina-Setup.exe**. Click it.

## Step 2 — The first warning (your browser)

Your browser may flag the download — Edge says something like
*"Lumina-Setup.exe was blocked because it could harm your device"* or
*"isn't commonly downloaded."*

**This is about popularity, not safety.** I'm one independent developer, not
a corporation, so my installer hasn't been downloaded a million times — and
that's the entire accusation. To keep it in Edge:

1. Find the download in the top-right corner (or press `Ctrl+J`)
2. Hover the download and click the **⋯** (three dots)
3. Click **Keep**
4. Click **Show more** on the panel that appears
5. Click **Keep anyway**

Chrome is similar: find the download, ⋯ menu, **Keep**.

## Step 3 — The second warning (Windows SmartScreen)

Run the installer, and Windows shows a blue box: *"Windows protected your
PC."* Same accusation, same reason — unknown publisher, not enough downloads
to have a reputation yet.

1. Click **More info**
2. Click **Run anyway**

That's the last warning. (It's the toll for buying from a person instead of
a corporation — I'd rather explain it honestly than pretend it won't happen.)

## Step 4 — The boring part

The installer itself is ordinary: Next, choose a folder or keep the default,
Install, Finish. Under a minute.

## Step 5 — Meet her setup wizard

Launch Lumina, and she takes over from here. The wizard walks you through
everything and waits patiently at every step — nothing proceeds without your
okay. What to expect:

- **The engine.** Her brain runs on a free engine called Ollama. The wizard
  sends you to download it, then watches for it. Once installed, look for a
  **little llama icon near your clock** — that means it's running. (Already
  have Ollama? She notices and skips ahead.)
- **The brain shelf.** She checks your computer's memory and tells you
  honestly which of her brains fit — the quick one, the smarter one, the
  deep thinker. Pick one; the download starts (2–5 GB, the longest wait of
  the day). If you're unsure, the recommended one is recommended for a
  reason.
- **While it downloads,** she asks her getting-to-know-you questions — her
  name, her manner, what you'd like her help with. Answer at your leisure;
  the download doesn't care.
- **Her voice (optional).** Six voices, each with a sample you can play.
  Saying yes downloads about 830 MB, once. You can skip this and add it
  later in Settings — she works fine in text.
- **Meet.** When her brain has fully arrived, you say hello.

## If something interrupts

Computer went to sleep? Closed the window by accident? Power blinked?
**Nothing is lost.** Relaunch Lumina — the wizard returns, skips what's
already done, and downloads resume from where they stopped, not from zero.
Change your mind about which brain mid-download? The back button lets you
swap; she cancels the old and starts the new.

## After setup

- **Your data lives in one ordinary folder** called `YourAI`, in your home
  folder — conversations, memories, notes, as readable files.
- **She's offline from here.** Weather and news are optional switches in
  Settings, off until you say otherwise, and the Settings page shows you
  exactly what touches the internet (spoiler: nothing, unless you switched
  it on).
- **Updates are offers, not requirements.** There's an update-check toggle,
  off by default. She works forever as installed either way.

## Trouble?

- **"Lumina is already running"** — she's telling the truth: check the
  system tray, or wait a minute and try again (her lights-off cleanup runs
  about 60 seconds after a window closes).
- **The wizard says the engine isn't running** — look for the llama near
  your clock; if it's missing, launch Ollama from the Start Menu.
- **Anything else:** hello@baer-dev.com. I built her and I answer my own
  mail. Tell me what you saw and I'll tell you what it means.
